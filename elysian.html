Elysian Siege

Platformer 2.5D Game

Description

The final project for my HND in Game Programming, developed over nine months by a multidisciplinary team of 16 members, including programmers, artists, and designers. Created in Unreal Engine 5, the game delivers roughly 90 minutes of immersive gameplay.
This is a fast-paced, dynamic 2.5D platformer where the player takes on the role of Keziah, a Celestial from the order of the Goddess of Nature. The player must venture deep into a temple, overcoming various challenges such as enemies, traps, and more.

The duration of this project is 10 months from September 2024 to June 2025.

My Tasks

Enemies

In this project, my main responsibility was focused on the enemies. I was in charge of creating a foundational base for all enemy types in the game (melee, tank, flying, and ranged), which included a patrol system following nodes placed throughout the level and a player detection system using Unreal's perception system. After that, I concentrated on the full development of the melee and tank enemies, handling both their behavior through the use of behavior trees and the integration of their animations.

Melee Enemy

The purpose of the melee enemy is to act as an obstacle for the player as they progress through the game, without becoming overly frustrating. Its behavior is based on patrolling an area and, upon detecting the player, quickly rushing in to attack. The player can defeat it either by jumping on it or by dashing it.

Tank Enemy

The tank enemy is the most challenging to defeat and is designed to present a challenge for the player. This enemy only appears in specific combat areas, where the player has no choice but to defeat it in order to progress. The player cannot damage this enemy until it reveals its weak point. The tank enemy approaches the player intending to strike with its mace. When it attacks, it slams the mace into the ground, releasing a shockwave. After finishing it's attack, the enemy briefly exposes its weak point, giving the player a window of opportunity to deal damage.

Combat Zone

As the player progresses through the game, they will encounter enemies, but they don't necessarily have to defeat them to move forward, except in specific areas called combat zones. When the player enters these zones, they will be locked in this area and they must defeat all the enemies there in order to continue.

Level Streaming and Optimization Volumes

For optimization purposes, a level streaming system and a optimization volumen system were created and then used by the designers. The level streaming system handles loading and unloading different areas of the game, helping to reduce resource consumption. The optimization volume system handles if different actors of the game should appear or not when the player revisits an area of the game, for example if the player collected all the pickups from an area so they don't appear again when revisiting it.
